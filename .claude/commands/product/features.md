**Features are the factual attributes, specifications, or characteristics of a product**, such as its size, color, materials, and technical details. In contrast, **benefits explain how those specific features directly improve the customer's life**, answering the fundamental question every buyer has: "What's in it for me?" or "What value would this bring to me?".

While it is important to highlight a product's factual features, **the actual selling points that motivate a purchase are found in the benefits**. Relying solely on technical specifications is a common mistake; instead, you should combine features and benefits to tell a compelling story that demonstrates exactly why the product is the perfect solution for the customer.

To clearly illustrate the difference between the two, consider these examples:
*   **Headphones:** A feature might be "noise-cancelling technology," but the actual benefit to the user is "immersive listening without interruptions".
*   **Office Chair:** A feature is a backrest made of an "advanced nylon weave". The resulting benefit is that this weave provides "stable yet flexible support for the back, encouraging good posture and making long days in front of the computer comfortable".
